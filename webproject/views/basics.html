Options Basics


Stock options are contracts that give the holder the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell a certain number of shares of a stock at a predetermined price within a specific time frame.

Call Options

A call option gives the holder the right to buy shares of stock at a predetermined price, known as the strike price. If the stock price goes up, the holder can exercise the option and buy the stock at the lower strike price, then sell it for a profit.

Put Options

A put option gives the holder the right to sell shares of stock at a predetermined price, known as the strike price. If the stock price goes down, the holder can exercise the option and sell the stock at the higher strike price, then buy it back for a profit.

Expiration Dates

Stock options have expiration dates, after which they become worthless. It's important to keep track of expiration dates and make decisions about whether to exercise options before they expire.

Risks and Rewards

Stock options can be a high-risk, high-reward investment. The potential rewards can be significant, but the risks of losing money are also high. It's important to understand the risks and rewards before investing in stock options.
