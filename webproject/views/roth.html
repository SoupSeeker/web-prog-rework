What Is a Roth IRA?


Roth IRA meaning

A Roth IRA(Indivdual Retirement Account) is an account that offers tax-free growth and tax-free withdrawals in retirement. A Roth IRA is a great choice for individuals who expect to be in a higher tax bracket in retirement then they currently are. It is also beneficial to those who want to diversify their retirement savings beyond traditional tax-deferred accounts.

Benefits of a Roth IRA

At retirement, the major benefit of a Roth IRA is that you will be able to withdraw your money without it ever being taxed. What your account has generated is all yours to keep.

Withdrawing Before 59.

Although it is not recommended to withdraw money, emergencies can happen. If you make any withdrawal before 59, you may have to pay taxes and penalties on those earnings.

Only withdraw the amount you need, if you withdraw too much, you may not be able to place it back into the account since contributions are capped at $6,000.00 a year.

When to open up a Roth IRA

The sooner the better. Unlike a 401k, contributions are capped at $6,000.00 a year, therefore time is the key to generating a fruitful retirement account that will be tax-free.
